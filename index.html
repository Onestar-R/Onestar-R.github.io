About

Ph.D. candidate in Supply Chain Management exploring how behavioral analytics, data-driven decision-making, network design and optimization can improve logistics and transportation systems. My research focuses on digital logistics, last-mile delivery, demand forecasting and assignment, and using generative models to support smarter logistics decisions, bridging theory with real-world applications.

Research Interests

Digital and Urban Logistics

Last-mile delivery, transportation network design, and Model context protocol

Operations research

Routing and allocation problems, traffic flow assignment, and heuristic optimization methods

Demand Forecasting and Analytics

Predicting demand patterns using time series and growth models

Behavior and big data analysis

Mode choice, analysis of freight rates and text data, and determinants of logistics decisions
