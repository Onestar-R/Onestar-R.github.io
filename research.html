Research Projects

Detailed overview of current and past research initiatives

Ongoing

Parcel Delivery Demand Analysis

2023 - Present

Overview

This research focuses on analyzing demand patterns in parcel delivery systems using large-scale data analysis techniques. The goal is to improve forecasting accuracy and optimize resource allocation in last-mile delivery operations.

Research Objectives

  • Develop accurate demand forecasting models for parcel delivery
  • Identify spatial and temporal patterns in delivery demand
  • Optimize resource allocation based on demand predictions
  • Improve operational efficiency in last-mile logistics

Methodology

Utilizing big data analytics, machine learning algorithms, and statistical modeling to process and analyze large-scale delivery data. The research employs time series analysis and spatial statistics to capture demand patterns.

Expected Impact

The findings will contribute to more efficient logistics operations, reduced delivery costs, and improved service quality in the parcel delivery industry.

Ongoing

Supply Chain Optimization

2022 - Present

Overview

Development of optimization models for supply chain networks with dual focus on efficiency and fairness. This research includes destination allocation models that balance route-income fairness with operational efficiency.

Research Objectives

  • Design fair and efficient destination allocation mechanisms
  • Balance multiple objectives in supply chain optimization
  • Develop mathematical models for route optimization
  • Consider both economic efficiency and social fairness

Key Contributions

This research introduces novel approaches to incorporate fairness considerations into traditional optimization frameworks, addressing the growing need for equitable solutions in logistics operations.

Applications

The developed models can be applied to various logistics scenarios including parcel delivery networks, freight transportation, and distribution systems.

Ongoing

Fair Labor Allocation in Logistics

2023 - Present

Overview

Investigation of fair labor allocation mechanisms in parcel delivery networks. This research addresses the critical balance between operational efficiency and equitable distribution of workload and income among delivery workers.

Research Questions

  • How to fairly distribute delivery routes among workers?
  • What metrics define fairness in labor allocation?
  • How to balance fairness with operational efficiency?
  • What are the impacts of fair allocation on worker satisfaction?

Approach

Combining operations research methodologies with fairness principles from economics and social sciences to develop allocation mechanisms that satisfy both efficiency and equity requirements.

Societal Impact

This research contributes to improving working conditions in the gig economy and platform-based delivery services by promoting fair treatment of delivery workers while maintaining service quality.
